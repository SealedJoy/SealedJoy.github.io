Frame Design: Cinematic UAV

Brief / Required Specs:

  • Guarded 3" Propellers for safety
  • Arm part can be used in any position
  • Ability to carry extra 120g payload
  • Mounts 12 x 12mm Motors
  • Mounts a 25.5mm VTX
  • Mounts a 30.5mm Flight Controller
  • Flexible action camera mounting

    • After performing extensive research into quadcopter designs, shortcomings and ideals. I designed this frame using Inkscape initially to sketch out. FreeCAD to adjust extrude and align the drawings into 3D parts. Utilising model slicing software I was able to accurately estimate the weights of parts in PETG plastic. After many adjustments; back and forth between programs the model was then printed on a FDM 3D printer over the course of two days.

    Maps: Structures

    This project aimed to produce a map showing 3D structures in smaller towns, where 3D structures are often not shown online. It involved using several obscure jar programs provided by the excellent OpenStreetMaps organisation. These programs allow for capturing a chosen area from the community built world map. It also allowed for granular control over cropping and removing elements of the map. I then exported these files into the 3D program Blender and begun cleaning, fixing and improving the mesh, removing many areas and features that would cause printing issues.

    It took several days of experimentation to get to the point where I had a correct model. The final result is quite charming and was given as a gift. However this product is now listed on Etsy as a customisable present.

    Transmitter Armor: Compact Hardcase

    Case designed for a Handheld Radio Transmitter This type of Radio transmitter is used widely within the RC world for hobbiest and professional use. The Case I designed was intended to provide maximum protection to the sensitive piece of equipment, whilst retaining it's compact form factor as a way to minimise the room required in a users bag. This makes it an attractive option for trekking to hard-to-reach filming and piloting locations.

    I utilised a 3D scanning app to produce a mesh of the Radiomaster Zorro Controller. After importing the scanned mesh, I used blender to sculpt around the controller. The scan, combined with several iterations of designs resulted in a very tight fit and reliable, minimal design. It also allowed me to be very accurate with cutouts, the design allows for extra thick and or long thumbsticks, it is compatible with existing mods and allows for USB access and charging.
